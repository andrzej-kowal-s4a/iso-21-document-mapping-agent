# Selected Documents for ISO 27001 Control 5.6 Contact with special interest groups

## 1. Security Incident Reporting Procedure / Procedura zgłaszania incydentów związanych z bezpieczeństwem
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104051

This procedure is highly relevant as it establishes the framework for security incident reporting and management, which directly supports the control's requirement for providing suitable liaison points when dealing with information security incidents. The procedure defines how security incidents are reported to the Security Team and managed through formal processes, ensuring that appropriate contacts and communication channels are established for incident response coordination with relevant parties.

## 2. Network General Security Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104105

This policy is directly relevant as it explicitly addresses the control's requirement for receiving early warnings of alerts, advisories and patches pertaining to attacks and vulnerabilities. The policy mandates that network devices have their vulnerabilities monitored by receiving security notifications from vendors, services, and advisories, demonstrating the organization's approach to staying informed about security threats through external sources and vendor relationships.

## 3. Vulnerability Management Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/214794244

This policy is relevant because it establishes processes for identifying and evaluating vulnerabilities, including assessment against known vulnerability databases. The policy demonstrates how the organization gains access to specialist information security advice and threat intelligence through vulnerability databases and external sources, which aligns with the control's objectives of staying current with security information and receiving early warnings about vulnerabilities.

## 4. Information Security Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104722

This policy is relevant as it establishes the organization's commitment to continuous improvement and staying current with the threat landscape. The policy explicitly states that Smart4Aviation will regularly review the effectiveness of the ISMS and update policies in response to changes in business context, threat landscape, and regulatory requirements. It also emphasizes aligning with industry best practices, which supports the control's requirement for improving knowledge about best practices and ensuring understanding of the information security environment is current.

## 5. Supplier Security Risk Management Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170099594

This policy is relevant because it establishes relationships with external parties (suppliers) and includes requirements for information sharing and security collaboration. The policy demonstrates how the organization engages with third parties to share and exchange information about security requirements, technologies, and best practices, which supports the control's objective of sharing and exchanging information about new technologies, products, services, threats or vulnerabilities through professional relationships.
