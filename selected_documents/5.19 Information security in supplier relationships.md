# Selected Documents for ISO 27001 Control 5.19 Information security in supplier relationships

## 1. Supplier Security Risk Management Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170099594

This is the primary policy document directly addressing the control requirements. It establishes comprehensive processes for managing supplier security risks including supplier evaluation, security requirements definition, RFI processes, contract negotiations with security clauses, periodic supplier audits through self-assessments, and supplier off-boarding procedures. The policy covers all core elements required by ISO 27001 Control 5.19, including identification of supplier types, evaluation and selection processes, risk assessment, monitoring compliance, incident handling, and secure termination of supplier relationships.

## 2. Company Managed Service Agreements
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170108066

This standard document provides framework for managing Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and Operational Level Agreements (OLAs) with suppliers and third parties. It supports control 5.19 by defining structured approaches to document service expectations, responsibilities, and performance metrics when engaging with suppliers providing IT services and underpinning contracts. The document ensures that supplier relationships are formalized with clear agreements that maintain agreed levels of information security.

## 3. License and hardware purchase or renewal process
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4AIH/pages/149520415

This procedure details the approval workflow for purchasing licenses and hardware from suppliers, including evaluation, manager approvals, and procurement team involvement for orders above certain thresholds. It provides operational implementation of supplier engagement controls and ensures appropriate oversight of supplier relationships throughout the procurement lifecycle. The procedure ensures that supplier purchases are subject to proper evaluation and approval processes, supporting the control's requirement for managing supplier security risks.

## 4. Information Security Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104722

This umbrella policy provides the foundational security framework that governs all information security practices, including supplier relationships. It establishes security objectives, information risk management principles, access management requirements, and customer data security provisions that must be applied when using supplier products and services. The policy ensures that supplier relationships are managed in accordance with the organization's overall information security requirements and risk management approach.

## 5. Information Classification Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170101881

This policy is critical for supplier relationships as it defines how information should be classified according to sensitivity levels (Secret, Private, Restricted, Sensitive, Disclosable). This classification scheme must be communicated to suppliers to ensure proper handling of shared information. The policy supports Control 5.19 by ensuring that information shared with suppliers is properly classified and that suppliers understand their obligations regarding information handling based on classification levels.
