# Selected Documents for ISO 27001 Control 5.32 Intellectual property rights

## 1. S4A License Tracking and Update Procedure
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104620

This document is directly relevant as it establishes procedures for tracking and maintaining software licenses, which is essential for intellectual property rights compliance. The procedure explicitly states its purpose is to protect Smart4Aviation's intellectual property and includes mechanisms for license expiration tracking, renewal processes, and maintaining license records in Jira. It addresses guidance points (d) maintaining asset registers, (e) maintaining proof of ownership of licenses, (f) ensuring license limits are not exceeded, and (h) procedures for maintaining appropriate licence conditions.

## 2. Endpoint Software Audit Procedure
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/1994686607

This procedure is highly relevant as it ensures compliance with ISO 27001 standards by systematically auditing software installed on company devices. It directly implements the requirement to carry out reviews ensuring that only authorized software and licensed products are installed (guidance point g). The procedure includes comparison against approved and prohibited software lists, monthly audits, and reporting mechanisms to ensure all installed software is properly licensed and authorized, preventing copyright infringement.

## 3. Legal and Regulatory Compliance Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170106479

This policy is relevant because it establishes the organization's commitment to compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, including those related to intellectual property rights. The policy explicitly covers legal obligations including permits, licences and other forms of authorisation, which directly supports guidance point (a) defining and communicating a topic-specific policy on protection of intellectual property rights. It ensures that Smart4Aviation operates within appropriate legal boundaries and monitors compliance with applicable laws in the Netherlands, Poland, and the European Union.

## 4. License and hardware purchase or renewal process
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4AIH/pages/149520415

This procedure is relevant as it establishes the proper process for acquiring software licenses through authorized channels, ensuring that software is only purchased through known and reputable sources (guidance point c). The procedure includes approval workflows, procurement team involvement for larger purchases, and proper documentation of license purchases. It ensures that copyright is not infringed upon by establishing a controlled process for license acquisition and renewal, maintaining proper records of all license purchases.

## 5. Supplier Security Risk Management Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170099594

This policy is relevant because it addresses supplier agreements and contractual security clauses that would include intellectual property rights provisions. The policy specifically covers purchases of licenses, license renewals, and subscriptions, requiring risk analysis and contractual provisions for appropriate security requirements. It ensures that supplier agreements include provisions for protecting intellectual property rights (guidance point b) and compliance with terms and conditions for software obtained from outside sources (guidance point j). The policy includes template contractual security clauses and requires NDA agreements when information is shared with suppliers.

## 6. Information Security Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104722

This policy is relevant because it establishes the requirement for maintaining inventory of hardware and software assets (guidance point d). The policy explicitly states that "Smart4Aviation Group shall maintain inventory of its hardware and software assets, at a level of granularity deemed necessary for tracking and reporting" and that IT assets shall have designated owners. This supports the intellectual property rights control by ensuring proper asset registers are maintained to identify all assets with requirements to protect intellectual property rights, enabling proper tracking and management of licensed software and proprietary information.
