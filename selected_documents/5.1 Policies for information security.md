# Selected Documents for ISO 27001 Control 5.1 Policies for information security

## 1. Information Security Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104722

This is the primary information security policy document that serves as the umbrella policy for all security policies across Smart4Aviation Group. It defines the organization's approach to managing information security, includes security objectives, assigns responsibilities, and establishes the framework for topic-specific policies. The document is approved by top management (CIO and other executives), has a defined review lifecycle (1 year or when important reasons arise), and explicitly links to all IT system policies, making it directly aligned with ISO 27001 control 5.1 requirements for an information security policy approved by management.

## 2. Policy Owner vs Author of procedures, standards, guidances and processes
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170102632

This policy establishes the framework for policy development, review, approval, and communication processes. It defines roles and responsibilities for Policy Owners and Authors, outlines the policy lifecycle including review cycles, approval processes, and communication to relevant personnel. The document ensures policies are developed with appropriate authority, reviewed at planned intervals, and communicated effectively, directly supporting ISO 27001 control 5.1 requirements for policy management, review, and communication.

## 3. Internal Auditing Policy for Security
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/2086699019

This policy establishes the framework for auditing the ISMS, including evaluation of internal normative documents (policies, procedures, standards, guidances) for relevance and compliance. It ensures policies are reviewed at planned intervals through audit programs, incorporates audit results into management reviews, and supports continual improvement of the ISMS. The policy directly addresses ISO 27001 control 5.1 requirement for policy review at planned intervals and when significant changes occur.

## 4. Information Classification Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170101881

This is a topic-specific policy that addresses information classification and handling, one of the example topics mentioned in ISO 27001 control 5.1. The policy is approved by appropriate level of management (CIO), linked to the Information Security Policy, and has a defined review lifecycle. It demonstrates how topic-specific policies are structured to address specific security areas while being aligned with and complementary to the main information security policy, fulfilling ISO 27001 control 5.1 requirements for topic-specific policies.

## 5. Risk Management Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170100789

This policy establishes the organization's approach to risk management, which is a fundamental component referenced in the Information Security Policy. It defines responsibilities, processes for risk identification and analysis, and ensures risk considerations are integrated into strategic planning. The policy supports ISO 27001 control 5.1 by ensuring the information security policy takes into account information security risks, as required by the control guidance.

## 6. Network General Security Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104105

This topic-specific policy addresses networking security, another example topic mentioned in ISO 27001 control 5.1. The policy is approved by appropriate management (CIO and technical director), linked to other related policies (Wireless Network Security Policy), and has a defined review cycle. It demonstrates how topic-specific policies are developed, approved, and maintained in alignment with the main information security policy, supporting ISO 27001 control 5.1 requirements.
