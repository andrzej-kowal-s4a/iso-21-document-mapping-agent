# Selected Documents for ISO 27001 Control 5.9 - Inventory of information and other associated assets

## 1. Endpoint Software Audit Procedure
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/1994686607

This procedure establishes systematic auditing of software installed on company devices, directly supporting the requirement to maintain inventories of software assets. It provides a monthly review process to ensure accurate tracking of software on endpoints, which is essential for maintaining an up-to-date software asset inventory and ensuring proper asset classification and ownership.

## 2. Hardware insurance list procedure
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4AIH/pages/334365211

This procedure directly addresses hardware asset inventory management by establishing a process to maintain lists of company-owned equipment that must be insured. It ensures that hardware assets are tracked with their location (KRK/GDN offices), gross replacement amounts, ID numbers, and production years, supporting the control's requirement for accurate and up-to-date asset inventories with location information.

## 3. Information Security Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104722

This overarching policy explicitly requires Smart4Aviation Group to maintain inventory of its hardware and software assets at an appropriate level of granularity for tracking and reporting. It mandates that IT assets be assigned to designated departments and that inventories be created and maintained automatically where technically feasible, directly supporting ISO 27001 Control 5.9 requirements.

## 4. Information Classification Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170101881

This policy supports Control 5.9 by establishing how information assets should be classified based on confidentiality, integrity, and availability levels. It mandates that information and information-processing assets have designated owners and provides detailed classification schemas, which directly supports the control's requirement that assets be classified according to information classification and have assigned ownership.

## 5. Server management procedure
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170107313

This comprehensive procedure manages the entire lifecycle of virtual machine and server assets from creation through decommissioning. It establishes processes for documenting server specifications, ownership, business justification, and expiration dates, supporting the control's requirements for maintaining accurate inventories of infrastructure assets with clear ownership and lifecycle management.

## 6. Employee Offboarding
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4AIH/pages/149520666

This procedure ensures that all IT assets are properly returned and removed from inventory when employees leave the organization. It includes detailed steps for recovering hardware, revoking licenses, and updating asset records, which is critical for maintaining accurate asset inventories and ensuring proper reassignment of asset ownership as required by Control 5.9.

## 7. License and hardware purchase or renewal process
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4AIH/pages/149520415

This procedure governs the acquisition of new hardware and software licenses, ensuring that all purchases are properly documented in JIRA and linked to appropriate license tickets. It supports asset inventory management by establishing a formal process for adding new assets to the organization's inventory with proper ownership assignment and tracking from the point of acquisition.

## 8. Disposal of electronic equipment [#hardware #utilisation] - procedure
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170108048

This procedure addresses the end-of-lifecycle stage for hardware assets, ensuring that disposed equipment is properly removed from inventory records and documented through utilization protocols. It supports Control 5.9's requirement that assets be removed from inventory when deleted or disposed, ensuring inventory accuracy throughout the complete asset lifecycle.
