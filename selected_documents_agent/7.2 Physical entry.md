# Selected Documents for ISO 27001 Control 7.2 Physical Entry

## 1. Office Alarm Code
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170107751

This procedure directly addresses physical entry control requirements by establishing the process for obtaining and using alarm codes for office access. It covers authentication mechanisms for physical access, access control procedures for securing premises in the morning and evening, and requirements for managing access authorization including the 6-month employment requirement before receiving an alarm code.

## 2. Alarm codes management
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4AIH/pages/149520699

This procedure supports Control 7.2 by providing detailed technical steps for managing alarm codes, including creating, modifying, and deleting user access codes. It addresses the authentication information management aspect of the control by documenting the process for key management (alarm codes), access authorization, and maintaining control over who has physical access to secure areas.

## 3. Procedura korzystnia z biura po godzinach pracy / After-Hours workplace activity procedure
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104529

This procedure is relevant to Control 7.2 as it establishes requirements for physical access management after normal business hours. It covers visitor management (requiring pre-approval for private events), supervision requirements, access authorization processes, restrictions on bringing unauthorized third parties, and the requirement that only staff with alarm codes can organize after-hours events.

## 4. Employee Offboarding
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4AIH/pages/149520666

This procedure supports Control 7.2 by ensuring the revocation of physical access rights when employees leave. It includes steps for deactivating alarm codes, removing employees from physical access systems, and revoking authentication credentials, which directly addresses the control's requirement for periodic review, update, and revocation of physical access authorizations.

**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104063

This procedure is relevant to Control 7.2 as it establishes the process for granting physical access rights to new employees. It covers identity verification (checking ID cards), provisioning of physical access credentials (alarm codes, keys), and the systematic approach to managing access to physical facilities from the moment employees join the organization.

## 6. Information Security Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104722

This high-level policy supports Control 7.2 by establishing the overall framework for physical security. It mandates that information processing facilities must have defined security perimeters, appropriate security barriers and access controls, visitor escort requirements, and protection from security threats, providing the policy foundation for all physical entry control procedures.
