# Selected Documents for ISO 27001 Control 5.19 Information security in supplier relationships

## 1. Supplier Security Risk Management Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170099594

This is the primary policy document directly addressing the control requirements. It establishes comprehensive processes for managing supplier security risks including supplier evaluation, security requirements definition, RFI processes, contract negotiations with security clauses, periodic supplier audits through self-assessments, and supplier off-boarding procedures - all core elements required by ISO 27001 Control 5.19.

## 2. Company Managed Service Agreements
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170108066

This standard document provides framework for managing Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and Operational Level Agreements (OLAs) with suppliers and third parties. It supports control 5.19 by defining structured approaches to document service expectations, responsibilities, and performance metrics when engaging with suppliers providing IT services and underpinning contracts.

## 3. License and hardware purchase or renewal process
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4AIH/pages/149520415

This procedure details the approval workflow for purchasing licenses and hardware from suppliers, including evaluation, manager approvals, and procurement team involvement for orders above certain thresholds. It provides operational implementation of supplier engagement controls and ensures appropriate oversight of supplier relationships throughout the procurement lifecycle.

## 4. Client contract termination checklist - customer offboarding
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/SUP/pages/160436260

While focused on customer offboarding, this procedure demonstrates structured termination processes including access revocation, data retrieval, environment shutdown, and secure data destruction. These practices are equally applicable to supplier off-boarding and align with Control 5.19's requirement for secure termination of supplier relationships including de-provisioning, information handling, and asset return.

## 5. Risk Management Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170100789

This policy establishes the overall risk management framework including risk identification, analysis, response planning, and monitoring. It supports Control 5.19 by providing the methodology for assessing and managing information security risks associated with supplier relationships, as referenced by the Supplier Security Risk Management Policy.

## 6. Information Security Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104722

This umbrella security policy defines Smart4Aviation's comprehensive approach to information security including access management, information classification, asset management, and security responsibilities. It provides the overarching security requirements framework that suppliers must align with, supporting the implementation of Control 5.19's requirement to maintain agreed security levels in supplier relationships.

## 7. Legal and Regulatory Compliance Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170106479

This policy ensures compliance with applicable laws and regulations across jurisdictions where Smart4Aviation operates. It supports Control 5.19 by establishing the legal and regulatory compliance expectations that must be communicated to and met by suppliers, particularly regarding data protection, contractual obligations, and regulatory requirements that affect supplier relationships.

## 8. Change Management Policy
**Confluence URL:** https://smart4aviation.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/S4A/pages/170104688

This policy defines structured processes for managing changes to IT services and infrastructure, including changes involving third-party providers and suppliers. It supports Control 5.19 by ensuring that changes related to supplier products, services, or infrastructure are properly planned, evaluated, approved, and documented to maintain service continuity and security.
